Zinédine Zidane:

Akhenaton:

Marcel Pagnol:

Born in the La Castellane neighborhoods of Marseille, Zinédine Zidane, nicknamed "Zizou," remains a legend in world football. His exceptional career, crowned by international successes such as the 1998 World Cup, elevated Marseille onto the global sports stage.

An emblematic figure in French rap, Akhenaton, whose real name is Philippe Fragione, has significantly contributed to the hip-hop scene. As a member of the IAM group, he propelled Marseille onto the map of French rap, using his voice to share profound reflections on society.

Writer and director Marcel Pagnol immortalized Provence in his work, capturing the very essence of Marseille life. Born in Aubagne, near Marseille, his timeless stories and films, such as "La Gloire de mon père" and "Manon des Sources," continue to fascinate generations.

Soprano:

JUL:

Ariane Ascaride:

Born in Marseille, Saïd M'Roumbaba, better known as Soprano, has conquered the world of music with his versatile talent. As a rapper, singer, and composer, Soprano has left an indelible mark on the French music industry with hits like "Cosmo" and "Roule." His influence extends beyond the realm of music, as he is also recognized for his social commitment, making him an inspiring figure for the youth.

Hailing from the Saint-Jean du Désert neighborhood in Marseille, Julien Mari, better known as JUL, is a phenomenon in French rap. His meteoric rise and impressive productivity in terms of albums and tracks have helped redefine the landscape of contemporary rap. JUL embodies the distinctive sound of the Marseille music scene, and his impact on French musical culture is undeniable, attracting a wide audience with his authentic lyrics and unique style.

Hailing from Marseille, Ariane Ascaride embodies excellence in the world of cinema and theater. With her prolific career, she has won numerous awards for her captivating performances, becoming an ambassador for Marseille's dramatic arts.
